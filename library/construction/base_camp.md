# Base Camp

## Description
<!-- @field: description -->

A base camp is a central location to which a group repeatedly returns for sleeping, food processing, tool production, and social activity. It represents the transition from continuous nomadic movement to anchored ranging, where foraging parties radiate outward from a fixed point and return with resources. The concept separates hominins from all other primates: chimpanzees nest in a new tree each night, but early Homo returned to the same site for days, weeks, or seasons. A base camp concentrates resources (tools, firewood, water proximity), reduces transport costs, provides a defensible location for vulnerable members (nursing mothers, injured, young children), and creates the spatial framework for division of labour. Without base camps, complex social organization is impossible.

## History
<!-- @field: history -->

The oldest candidate for a base camp is FLK Zinj at Olduvai Gorge (Tanzania, 1.75 Ma), excavated by Mary and Louis Leakey from 1959. This site contains dense concentrations of stone tools and butchered animal bones across a 300 m2 area, with spatial patterning suggesting repeated use. Glynn Isaac (1978) proposed the "home base hypothesis", arguing that sites like Koobi Fora (Kenya, 1.6 Ma) show food carried from kill sites to central locations for group sharing, a behaviour with no primate parallel. At Gesher Benot Ya'aqov (Israel, 790,000 BP), Naama Goren-Inbar found evidence of repeated fire use at the same location across multiple occupation layers, confirming long-term site fidelity. The DK site at Olduvai Gorge (1.8 Ma) contains a stone circle 4 m in diameter that Mary Leakey interpreted as the base of a windbreak or shelter, making it possibly the oldest architectural structure known.

## Procedure
<!-- @field: procedure -->

**Site selection criteria (in order of priority):**

| Priority | Criterion | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Water within 200 m | Daily water trips must be short and safe |
| 2 | High ground or slope | Water drainage, visibility, defensibility |
| 3 | Wind shelter | Rock face, dense treeline, or terrain fold to block prevailing wind |
| 4 | Escape routes | Minimum 2 directions to flee if threatened |
| 5 | Firewood supply | Deadfall within 100 m radius for multi-day stays |
| 6 | Clear sightlines | Ability to see approaching predators/groups from 50+ m |

**Establishing a base camp:**

1. Walk the candidate site completely before committing. Check for animal tracks (predator lairs), insect nests (especially ants and wasps), and flood indicators (debris lines on trees).
2. Clear a central area 4-6 m in diameter. Remove sharp rocks, branches, and loose debris.
3. Establish the hearth at the centre or slightly upwind of the sleeping area. The fire serves as heat source, predator deterrent, light, and cooking station.
4. Designate zones: sleeping area (downwind of fire, on softest ground), tool-making area (near raw material pile, away from sleeping zone to avoid flake injuries), food processing area (separate to control waste).
5. Build a windbreak on the windward side using stacked branches, large stones, or piled brush. Height of 1-1.5 m is sufficient.
6. Establish a latrine area 30+ m downwind and downhill from camp. Consistent use prevents disease and predator attraction.
7. Create a firewood cache. Collect 2 days' worth immediately. Never let it drop below one day's supply.

**Maintenance cycle (daily):**

1. Morning: stoke fire, assign daily roles, dispatch foraging teams.
2. Midday: process incoming food, tend fire, maintain tools.
3. Evening: all members return before dusk, evening meal, fire built up for night.
4. Night: rotating watch in 2-hour shifts. Minimum one person awake at all times.

## Applications
<!-- @field: applications -->

- **Food processing hub:** Butchering, marrow extraction, plant grinding, and drying all require stable surfaces and tools left in place. A base camp eliminates the need to carry heavy processing equipment during foraging.
- **Tool workshop:** Knapping produces sharp debris. A dedicated area with raw material stockpiles allows sustained tool production without risk to sleeping areas.
- **Childcare centre:** Infants and toddlers remain at camp with dedicated caregivers rather than being carried on foraging trips, reducing caloric cost for mothers and expanding their foraging range.
- **Recovery station:** Injured or sick group members have shelter, water access, fire, and food delivery without needing to travel.
- **Social anchor:** Camp is where information exchanges, conflict resolution, teaching, and planning happen. It is the physical foundation of group cohesion.

## Day 0 - Cookbook
<!-- @field: day0_cookbook -->

Stop wandering. Pick a spot and make it yours. You need water close, high ground if you can get it, something solid at your back to block the wind, and clear lines of sight so nothing walks up on you unseen.

Walk the entire area first. Look down. Animal tracks leading to a depression mean something sleeps here. Ant columns mean a nest underground. Flood debris on tree trunks at ankle height means this spot fills with water when it rains. Move on if any of these are present.

Clear a circle about five paces across. Throw out every sharp stone and branch. Build your fire at the centre. Ring it with stones so embers do not spread. Stack firewood on the upwind side, enough for two days. You will burn through more than you expect.

Pile brush or branches into a wall on the windward side, as high as your waist. It does not need to be beautiful. It needs to block the wind that steals your body heat at 3 AM.

Pick a toilet area 30 paces downhill and downwind. Everyone uses it. Every time. Scattered waste attracts predators and breeds disease.

Assign one person to stay at camp at all times. They tend the fire, watch for threats, process whatever food comes in, and keep the children alive. This is the most important job in camp. Never leave it unmanned.

Come back to this exact spot every night. Your tools stay here. Your firewood stays here. Your sleeping insulation stays here. Every night you do not have to rebuild from nothing is a night you saved two hours of energy. That energy keeps you alive.
